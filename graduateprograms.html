Master’s Degree Programs

Master of Science in Data Science and Analytics

Program Overview

The MSc in Data Science and Analytics provides advanced training in data handling, predictive modeling, and statistical inference for real-world problem-solving. This program is designed for students who want to develop expertise in data interpretation, machine learning, and business intelligence. The curriculum balances theoretical grounding with practical exposure to tools such as Python, R, Spark, and SQL.


Learners work on data-driven research projects and capstone assignments that apply to domains like health informatics, financial forecasting, and public policy. Graduates are well-positioned to become data scientists, quantitative analysts, or strategic decision-makers in corporate, governmental, and nonprofit settings.

Master of Science in Artificial Intelligence and Intelligent Systems

Program Overview

This graduate program delves into the design and deployment of intelligent agents, autonomous systems, and cognitive computing. The MSc in Artificial Intelligence and Intelligent Systems covers deep learning, knowledge representation, computer vision, robotics, and ethics in AI. Students engage in lab-based research and prototyping using platforms like TensorFlow, ROS, and PyTorch.


The program emphasizes both theoretical and applied approaches to AI, with opportunities for industrial collaboration and advanced research. Graduates are equipped to pursue careers in AI development, smart automation, algorithm engineering, or doctoral research in human-centered AI design.

Master of Science in Machine Learning Engineering

Program Overview

The MSc in Machine Learning Engineering focuses on the development, scaling, and deployment of intelligent systems capable of learning from data. Students learn how to engineer production-grade ML systems, optimize model performance, and work with large-scale data pipelines. Core topics include supervised/unsupervised learning, probabilistic models, model interpretability, and cloud-based deployment.


This program is ideal for students with strong programming and mathematical backgrounds seeking careers as ML engineers, data architects, or AI product developers. Practical modules emphasize engineering rigor, reproducibility, and real-time model monitoring in industries such as fintech, bioinformatics, and smart infrastructure.

Master of Engineering in Intelligent Systems

Program Overview

A blend of engineering design and intelligent computing, this program prepares students to build cyber-physical systems with embedded AI capabilities. The Master of Engineering in Intelligent Systems includes coursework in autonomous robotics, IoT integration, smart control systems, and embedded machine learning.


Students undertake collaborative projects across disciplines to design solutions for industrial automation, energy management, and intelligent mobility. Graduates typically work as intelligent systems engineers, robotics developers, or innovation consultants in tech-driven industries seeking real-time automation solutions.

Master of Computational Statistics and Predictive Analytics

Program Overview

This program integrates high-level statistical theory with applied predictive analytics for decision-making. Students explore regression modeling, time-series analysis, Bayesian methods, and statistical programming with a focus on interpreting complex datasets. Tools such as R, SAS, and STATA are used alongside real-world case studies.


Graduates emerge with expertise in designing experiments, modeling risk, and generating actionable insights from structured and unstructured data. Career pathways include statistical consultancy, research analysis, actuarial science, or advanced analytics roles in sectors like insurance, government, and logistics.

Doctoral (PhD) Degree Programs

PhD in Artificial Intelligence

Program Overview

The PhD in Artificial Intelligence at Educorp University is a research-intensive program focused on advancing the frontiers of AI theory, systems, and applications. Doctoral candidates explore areas such as autonomous systems, deep neural networks, knowledge representation, reinforcement learning, and ethical AI. The program encourages original contributions through research publications, prototype development, and interdisciplinary collaborations.


Students work closely with faculty mentors, research labs, and industry partners to tackle complex, real-world challenges across domains like healthcare, smart mobility, and cybersecurity. Graduates of this program are prepared for careers as AI researchers, policy advisors, university faculty, or senior innovation leads in AI-driven organizations.

PhD in Machine Learning and Human-Centered Computing

Program Overview

This PhD program bridges cutting-edge machine learning techniques with human-centric design principles to build systems that are ethical, interpretable, and socially impactful. Areas of focus include explainable AI, interactive learning systems, adaptive algorithms, and fairness in algorithmic decision-making. Students are expected to design and evaluate ML systems with a strong emphasis on usability, accessibility, and equity.


The program blends computer science, cognitive psychology, and design thinking, producing scholars capable of leading cross-functional teams in academia, tech, or government. Graduates are uniquely positioned to guide the development of machine learning technologies that prioritize transparency and inclusivity.

PhD in Data-Driven Decision Sciences

Program Overview

The PhD in Data-Driven Decision Sciences is designed for scholars interested in advancing the use of data for strategic, evidence-based decision-making. Research areas include optimization models, decision theory, operations research, computational economics, and behavioral analytics. Candidates develop expertise in using data to inform policies, investment strategies, and resource allocation in complex environments.


With applications spanning healthcare systems, public policy, logistics, and financial planning, this PhD program produces highly analytical thinkers ready to shape high-stakes decision environments. Graduates often pursue roles as lead analysts, policy scientists, or academic researchers in quantitative disciplines.

PhD in Computational Intelligence

Program Overview

The PhD in Computational Intelligence focuses on biologically inspired computation, hybrid AI models, and intelligent optimization algorithms. Students conduct advanced research in evolutionary algorithms, swarm intelligence, fuzzy systems, and neural computation. The program supports both theoretical innovation and real-world application in fields such as robotics, simulation, and adaptive control systems.


Doctoral candidates benefit from interdisciplinary collaboration with departments in biology, engineering, and cognitive science, enabling them to build complex adaptive systems. Graduates often contribute to next-generation technologies and publish in leading AI and systems journals.

PhD in Applied Data Science for Emerging Technologies

Program Overview

This innovative program prepares researchers to use advanced data science techniques to address challenges in emerging technologies such as IoT, renewable energy systems, smart cities, and blockchain. Research spans predictive analytics, distributed systems, edge computing, and data ethics in tech innovation. Students are encouraged to work on cross-cutting projects that have real-world societal impact.


With access to Educorp’s high-performance computing infrastructure and partner innovation hubs, candidates develop data-driven solutions with measurable outcomes. The program produces domain experts and research leaders equipped to guide the evolution of intelligent, data-powered systems.
